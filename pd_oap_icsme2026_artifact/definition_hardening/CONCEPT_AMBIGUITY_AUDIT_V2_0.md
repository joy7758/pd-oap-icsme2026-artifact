# Concept Ambiguity Audit v2.0

| Ambiguous phrase in v1.x | Why a reviewer may object | Replacement definition or wording | Where to apply in v2.0 manuscript |
|---|---|---|---|
| benchmark | May sound like a population-scale or statistical benchmark. | controlled fixture benchmark: a bounded executable suite for representation and diagnostic boundary testing. | Title, abstract, evaluation, threats. |
| validation | Could imply legal, operational, or external correctness. | validation of structure, reference closure, declared linkage, and expected failure diagnosis. | Introduction, method, evaluation. |
| reviewability | Could be read as legal audit completion or human approval. | ability to reconstruct and inspect one operation from a packaged statement. | Definitions, same-case comparison, discussion. |
| public-data operation | Could be mistaken for an entire data portal or governance program. | one bounded access, reuse, or review decision over a public-data object. | Problem scope, definitions, fixture design. |
| evidence | Could imply court-grade proof or complete external evidence. | packaged evidence references sufficient for structural inspection within the stated boundary. | Profile, artifact availability, threats. |
| accountability | Could imply institutional or legal accountability. | artifact-level operation accountability through checkable statement structure and non-claims. | Introduction, definitions, contribution list. |
| profile | Could be read as only a schema. | a profile-and-checker method that specifies statement structure, validation expectations, and failure surfaces. | Introduction, method, related work. |
| auditor | Could imply a human regulator or official audit body. | an independent local auditor implementation used for cross-checking expected fixture outcomes. | Checker/auditor section, evaluation. |
| independent | Could imply external implementation evidence beyond the local artifact package. | independently implemented within the local artifact package to cross-check checker outputs. | Method, evaluation, threats. |
| empirical validation | Could be read as real-world population validation. | RQ-driven artifact evaluation using expanded fixtures, mutation evaluation, raw counts, and agreement analysis. | Evaluation section and venue framing. |
